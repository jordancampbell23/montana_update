Revenue vs. Poverty Rate

Overall, there is not a statistically significant correlation between state and local revenue per pupil and student poverty. While this means that districts with higher concentrations of students in poverty don’t typically receive less funding, it also means that Tennessee’s funding system isn’t successfully targeting additional state and local dollars to these students.

Revenue vs. Property Wealth

There is a moderate positive correlation between state and local revenue per pupil and local property wealth when looking at county (non-municipal) districts. It’s important for policymakers to keep this in mind when assessing Tennessee’s funding system.

Revenue by Property Wealth Quartile

The relationship between state and local revenue per pupil and total local property assessment per pupil is apparent as revenue increases in lock-step with property wealth across quartiles. On average, county districts with higher property wealth (i.e. 4th quartile districts) receive $1,657 more per student than the lower property wealth county districts (i.e. 1st quartile districts).
